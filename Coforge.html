⚠ Zero Promoter Holding — Structurally Professional
Coforge has 0% promoter holding as it was spun off from NIIT and is now a widely held professional company. This is not a negative signal — it means management is accountable to institutional shareholders. Combined FII+DII holding of ~62% provides strong governance oversight. Unlike owner-operated companies, there is no promoter pledging risk. However, without a large promoter stake, hostile takeover risk is theoretically higher (mitigated by strong institutional ownership).
⚠ Debtor Days at 62 — Above Ideal but Sector-Normal
Coforge's debtor days of 62 exceed the ideal benchmark of <30 days, but this is common in IT services where large enterprise clients have 45-90 day payment cycles. The trend has been improving from ~75 days in FY21. Working capital days of 38 days is manageable. Cash conversion remains healthy at ~75% of EBITDA.
⚠ Cigniti Merger Integration Risk
The FY25 acquisition of Cigniti Technologies (a quality assurance/testing firm) has elevated consolidated debt (D/E rose from 0.15x to 0.42x) and added integration complexity. Revenue synergies are projected but not yet fully proven. FY26 results showed strong 91.6% profit growth — early signs of successful integration, but full realisation will take 2-3 years. Monitor quarterly revenue growth and margin stability for integration success.

📖 Definitions — tap to expand

P/E ratio — What you pay per ₹1 of profit. Lower vs history and sector = cheaper.

PEG Ratio — P/E divided by earnings growth rate. Below 1 = growth available at a reasonable price.

P/B ratio — Price vs net assets. Below 1 = buying at a discount to book value.

P/Sales — Price vs revenue. Useful when profits are cyclical or recovering.

EV/EBITDA — Full business value check including debt. Lower = better.

Earnings Yield — EPS / CMP. Compare to risk-free rate (~7%).

ROE — Profit generated per ₹100 of shareholders' equity. Above 15% = good.

ROCE — How efficiently total capital is used. Above 15% = healthy.

FCF — Cash left after all expenses and investments. Positive and growing = genuinely healthy.

Debtor Days — Average time to collect payment. Below 30 days = excellent.

Piotroski Score — 0–9 financial health score. 7–9 = strong.

RSI — Relative Strength Index. Below 30 = oversold; above 70 = overbought.

MACD — Shows relationship between two EMAs. Bullish crossover = positive signal.

VWAP — Volume-Weighted Average Price for the session.

Delivery % — Shares resulting in delivery. Higher = more conviction buying.

ADX — Trend strength. Above 25 = strong trend; below 20 = weak/choppy.

CAGR — Compound Annual Growth Rate over N years.

Promoter pledging — Founders borrowing against shares. Above 10% = red flag.
